1. What was Mahatma Gandhi's method of mass agitation called and what did it emphasize?
2. In which years did Mahatma Gandhi organize satyagraha movements in Champaran, Kheda, and Ahmedabad?
3. What was the purpose of Mahatma Gandhi's nationwide satyagraha against the Rowlatt Act and what were some of the consequences of this movement?%%%%1. Mahatma Gandhi's method of mass agitation was called satyagraha and it emphasized the power of truth and the need to search for truth. It suggested that physical force was not necessary to fight the oppressor and that non-violence could win the battle.
2. Mahatma Gandhi organized satyagraha movements in Champaran in Bihar in 1917, in the Kheda district of Gujarat in 1917, and in Ahmedabad in 1918.
3. The nationwide satyagraha against the Rowlatt Act was organized by Mahatma Gandhi in 1919. The purpose of this satyagraha was to protest against the Act, which gave the government enormous powers to repress political activities and allowed detention of political prisoners without trial for two years. The consequences of this movement included rallies, strikes, and hartals taking place in various cities, leading to the clamp down on nationalists by the British administration and the imposition of martial law.
